Definitions

  1. Introduction to Operator & Agents (OpenAI, Jan-2025)
    • "AI agents are AI systems that can do work for you independently; you give them a task, and they go off and do it"
  2. What are AI agents? (IBM, Jul-2024)
    • "AI agents are compound AI systems where an LLM is in charge of the control logic"
  3. Introduction to Agents (Hugging Face, Jan-2025)
    • "AI Agents are programs where LLM outputs control the workflow"
  4. Building effective agents (Anthropic, Dec-2024)
    • "AI Agents are systems where LLMs dynamically direct their own processes and tool usage"
  5. Agents (Chip Huyen, Jan-2025)
    • "AI is the brain that processes, plans, and determines whether a task has been accomplished"
  6. Introducing ambient agents (Langchain, Jan-2025)
    • "Ambient agents listen to an event stream and act on it accordingly, potentially acting on multiple events at a time"

Classifications

  1. By how they are BUILT: Code (or Framework) vs. Assisted Code (Chat) vs. No-code (UI)
  2. By the core APPROACH: Graph-based (e.g. LangGraph) vs. Objective-driven (e.g. Smolagents)
  3. By how they are USED: Code (or API) vs. UI
  4. By how they REASON: in Language vs. in Code (e.g. Smolagents CodeAgent)
  5. By how they are TRIGGERED: Reactive (when called) vs. Proactive (Ambien agents)
  6. By their AMBITION: Automate something we were already doing vs. Explore doing something new
  7. By their TARGET: B2B (Enterprise vs. SMB; vertical by industry or function) vs. B2C / Consumer
